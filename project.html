An Investigation into World Poverty and Inequality :

Global inequality and poverty are complex issues that affect individuals and societies across the world. They are the result of a variety of factors, including economic, political, and social systems, as well as historical and cultural factors.

This project explores the recent trends, examining the relationships between key indicators to better further our understanding of what needs to change for a more equal world.

Chart 1 - World Inequality

The map below illustrates Worldwide GINI coefficient for the past 34 years. Through implementation of a slider, it allows the initial lack of information on some countries to be supplemented through scrolling at historical values.

One country I found of note to look at was Brazil, who's GINI stayed at 50% between the first year and last, indicating not much overall development has happened with them being stuck in relative poverty. Another interesting thing was the USA, which hovered at a GINI of 40% for the past thirty years. This could indicate that wealth distribution must not be very equal.

Chart 2 - Are GDP and Literacy Rate correlated?

This chart looks at the relationship between the GDP per capita and Literacy Rate of poorer countries. I have set a maximum value of $22,000, as I want to focus on LEDCs and have a better readable graph.

By using a Population size legend, I can show larger countries more prominently.

My regression value of 0.29 indicates some correlation, however not an extremely strong one. I believe this can be explained to outliers such as China which has a GDP per capita of 13000 and a Literacy Rate of 0.97.

Chart 3 -Is there a Poverty Trap?

This chart looks at whether countries that have historically low GDP per capita are able to improve their situation. Something notable is Ireland that started with a relatively low GDP but managed to grow into a rich country

We can see that countries that have historically high GDP relative, to begin with, will generally make more GDP per annum. However, countries that have historically low GDPs such as Uganda will remain around the same, indicating that the standard of living in the country has still not increased significantly.

Chart 4 - The Relationship between Human Development Index and Gender Inequality

I used a scatter plot to see the correlation between these variables clearly :

By using socio-economic indicators, such as HDI & GII, it is possible to see a close relationship between the two figures for the dataset. With this line of bests fit regression evenly through, we can sese a clear correlation between the two as a higher developed countries have less gender inequality.

Chart 5 -What's the relationship between Health Expenditure and Life Expectancy?
